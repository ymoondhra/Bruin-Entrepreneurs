about

Designathon is a beginner-friendly, experience design competition hosted by Bruin Entrepreneurs with the goal of fostering design thinking at UCLA through a series of workshops, culminating in a final competition. The event will be held in Carnesale Commons in the Palisades Room. Find out more about last year's designathon here!

what is design thinking?

According to IDEO, design thinking utilizes elements from the designer's toolkit like empathy and experimentation to arrive at innovative solutions.

check-in

Pastries and coffee will be provided for breakfast as participants check in for the event.

design challenge

We will start with a ten minute overview of the day and jump into an intro to design thinking and its applications to experience design and related fields. We will also reveal the prompt and judging rubric for the final project. Team formation will be conducted after the ceremony.

empathizing with Northrop Grumman

Northrop Grumman, a defense technology and aerospace company, will be hosting a workshop on the first two steps of the design thinking process: empathizing with the user and defining the problem. Participants will learn how to craft personas and user journeys. Learn more about the workshop here.

think break

During this break, participants have a chance to visit Nate Mohler’s VR art installation, paint, or take a pic with your friends at our photobooth!

ideating with Ticketmaster

Ticketmaster, a ticket sales and distribution company, will be hosting a workshop on that will elaborate on the ideation part of the first workshop. Participants will learn how to make wireframes and mockups. Learn more about the workshop here.

lunch on the hill

Lunch will be provided by De Neve dining hall. There will be mini workshops for Sketch and Adobe Xd.

prototyping with creative labs

Creative Labs will be hosting a workshop on prototyping a product. Participants will be learning how to turn their mockup into an interactive model of their envisioned product using Figma. Learn more about the workshop here.

design time

During this work time, you will have a chance to consolidate all the deliverables (personas, mockups, prototypes) that you will have made in the workshops into a final project. You will have flexibility in the form in which you choose present your project.

design in industry panel

We have design industry leaders Chris Alonzo, Chengsu Chen, Jeff Morris, and Kelly Wald speaking on our Design in Industry panel. The panel will be moderated by Tiffany Yu and Natasha Puthukudy, followed by the audience asking questions during the second half of the panel. Judging for final projects will be happening simultaneously as the panel is happening.

closing ceremony

The closing ceremony will include announcing winners, having the winners present their projects, and some concluding thoughts on design thinking and the competition.

design thinking faq

With the rise of technology, design thinking is a problem solving toolkit that puts humans and empathy at the forefront of products and innovation. Design thinking informs human-centered innovation by minimizing the uncertainty and risk of innovation by engaging customers or users through a series of prototypes to learn, test and refine concepts. Design thinkers rely on customer insights gained from real-world experiments, not just historical data or market research.

Design thinking is a human-centered problem solving approach that is permeating every industry, including fields such as medicine, engineering, and education. It is thus is limitless in its applications. For a more in-depth look at its use in a real world context, this Forbes article provides some examples.
